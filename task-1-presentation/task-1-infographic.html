What is Digital Literacy?

  • Definition: The ability to find, evaluate, create, and share information using digital tools responsibly.
  • Not just coding: Sending a professional email, spotting fake news, and using Google Forms all count.
  • 4 Core Skills: Information literacy, Online communication, Digital safety, Problem-solving with tech.
  • Why it matters: Every career requires digital skills — from internship applications to remote teamwork.
  • Lifelong skill: As technology changes, digital literacy helps you adapt and stay relevant.

Stay Protected

Safe Internet Practices

  • Strong passwords: Use 12+ characters with letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords.
  • Spot phishing: Fake emails create urgency to make you click suspicious links. Always verify the sender.
  • UPI safety: Entering your PIN sends money OUT — not in. Never share OTPs with anyone.
  • Update software: Regular updates patch security vulnerabilities that hackers exploit.
  • Report crimes: Cybercrime helpline 1930 and cybercrime.gov.in are available 24×7.

Build Your Brand

Professional Online Presence

  • LinkedIn profile: Add your degree, institution, and skills. Employers check profiles before interviews.
  • GitHub portfolio: Showcase projects and code. A profile README shows initiative and professionalism.
  • Think before posting: Public posts are permanent. Avoid content that could affect your reputation.
  • Consistent identity: Use the same professional photo and name across all platforms.
  • Engage wisely: Follow industry leaders, share learning, and comment thoughtfully online.
