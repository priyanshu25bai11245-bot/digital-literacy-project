# Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where
scammers trick victims into sending money by exploiting their lack of awareness
about how UPI payment requests work.

## How It Typically Happens – Step by Step

1. The victim posts something for sale on an online marketplace like OLX or Quikr.
2. A scammer contacts them pretending to be an interested buyer.
3. The scammer says they will send payment via UPI and shares a QR code or
   sends a "collect request" on apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm.
4. The victim, believing they are about to receive money, scans the QR code
   or accepts the collect request and enters their UPI PIN.
5. Instead of receiving money, the victim's account is instantly debited —
   because entering a UPI PIN always authorises sending money, never receiving it.
6. The scammer disappears immediately after the transaction is completed.

## Who is Usually Targeted?
- First-time online sellers, especially students and young adults
- People in rural or semi-urban areas with limited digital literacy
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with UPI mechanics
- Anyone in urgent need of money who may act without thinking carefully

## Consequences
- Immediate financial loss — often ranging from a few hundred to several
  thousand rupees
- Victims have little legal recourse as transactions are authorised by them
- Psychological distress, loss of trust in digital payment systems
- In severe cases, victims take loans or fall into debt due to repeated fraud

## Source
This scenario is based on widely reported UPI fraud patterns documented by
the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal
(cybercrime.gov.in).
